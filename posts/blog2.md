### Choosing a Community Platform
For this project, my group had decided to focus on the **badminton community**. This decision was mutually made as we all had a shared interest in badminton and is also based on the nature of the sport itself, which involves continuous skill development and peer learning. Unlike more passive interest based communities, badminton players often seek feedback, training opportunities and team sports to improve their performance. 

Within a badminton community, users are not only interested in sharing content, but also in improving their skills and learning from others. Some key needs include:

- Receiving feedback on technique (e.g. footwork, grip, timing)  
- Learning from more experienced players  
- Sharing progress and training experiences  
- Engaging in discussions that go beyond general conversation  

However, existing platforms such as Reddit or Facebook groups tend to support unstructured discussion, which can make it difficult for users to receive clear and actionable feedback.

### Feature Ideation
To respond to these needs, my group explored broader system structures for our community map. This included multiple interconnected sections such as events, forums, member profiles and a courts map.

![Badminton Community System](DECO2017-Blogs/posts/images/sitemap.png)
**Figure 1. Overall system structure of the badminton community hub**


Based on this, we decided to focus on the **events sytem** as a standout feature as it directly supports the primary goal of badminton players which is being able to participate in games and improving their experiences. However, instead of listing a standard event listing system, the design introduces a **skill based event matching approach**.
This includes:
- Displaying events categorised by skill level (e.g beginner, intermediate, advanced).
- Providing visibility into session participation (display number of attendees).
- Supporting more balanced and appropriate grouping of players.

This turns events into a decision support tool which helps users find sessions that are more suitable for their ability level.

### Design Decisions
Focusing on the events system required prioritising depth over breadth. While other features such as the courts map and forum remain part of the system, they function as supporting components rather than the main interaction.

This reflects a trade-off between:
- Expanding multiple general features  
- Refining one feature that provides meaningful value  

Additionally, technical constraints and performance requirements limit how complex the system can be implemented.

This process highlights that designing a community platform is not about including as many features as possible, but about identifying where the most value can be created. By focusing on the events system and refining it to better support user needs, the platform becomes more purposeful and aligned with the goals of BlaBla Corp.
