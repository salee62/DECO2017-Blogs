### Design Decisions
After establishing that we wanted to make badminton community our focus and identifying the events system as the platform's standout feature, we then moved onto exploring how users would interact with the platform. The first step we took is our group all sketched a series of low fidelity wireframes of the main pages to test different layouts, interaction priorities and navigation structures as well as see where we were on the same page and instigate different ideas. 

The low fidelity pages were used to explore how different features could be prioritised throughout the platform. Some layouts focused more on forums and community discussions while others prioritised event discovery, maps or profile interactions. This process helped our group identify which interactions felt more valuable and relevant.

**Figure 1. Early low fidelity wireframes exploring different layouts and interactions**

After we all sketched the main pages, we pin point features we liked from each other's pages with a star to input into our final sketch. This stage highlighted that event-based interaction created the strongest opportunities for engagement. Since badminton players are primarily looking for opportunities to participate in games and connect with players of similar skill levels, event discovery consistently became the central focus across multiple wireframe variations.

### Interaction Design

Following the low fidelity wireframes, we then developed high fidelity wireframes to refine interaction patterns and improve usability. This allowed us to focus on creating a clearer and more polished event experience.

<img src="assets/images/high-wireframes.png" alt="Badminton Community System" width="90%">

**Figure 2. High fidelity screens**

Several refinements were introduced during this stage. Event cards became more structured and visually consistent, making it easier for users to quickly scan important information such as skill level, date, location, and attendance. Skill level tags were also made more visible to support the platform’s focus on helping users identify suitable sessions. The search and filtering system was refined to support faster navigation and event discovery. Rather than overwhelming users with excessive information, the interface prioritises key details that directly influence participation decisions.This stage also introduced stronger visual hierarchy through clearer spacing, card layouts, and interaction elements, improving readability and usability across the platform.

Another major design focus involved refining the RSVP and attendee interaction flow. Rather than treating RSVP as a simple confirmation system, the design aimed to make participation feel more socially connected and community-driven. The high-fidelity wireframes introduced attendee visibility, allowing users to view who is attending a session before confirming participation. Profile pop-ups and participant displays help users better understand the session environment and encourage stronger social interaction between members. Additionally, the RSVP confirmation flow was simplified to reduce unnecessary friction and create a smoother interaction experience. This helps support quicker decision-making while keeping the process intuitive and accessible.

### Interface Interaction

While the event system remained the primary focus, supporting features such as the courts map, forum, and profiles were still included to strengthen the overall platform experience. However, an important design decision involved ensuring that these features supported the event interaction system rather than competing with it. The courts map became a supporting discovery tool, while the forum acts as a secondary space for community discussion and interaction. This helped maintain a clearer platform focus and prevented the system from becoming overloaded with too many competing priorities.

This iteration process demonstrated the importance of exploring multiple interaction approaches before refining the final direction. Through both low-fidelity and high-fidelity wireframes, the project evolved from a broader community platform into a more focused event-driven experience tailored specifically for badminton players.

The refinement of event discovery, skill-based participation, and RSVP interactions helped strengthen the platform’s standout feature while improving usability and clarity throughout the interface.
